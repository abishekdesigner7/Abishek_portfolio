Case Study

Cafe Gokulam

Designing a Warm & Inviting Restaurant Experience

Role UI/UX Designer
Timeline 1 Week
Tools Figma, Claude AI
Type Restaurant Website
Overview

Project Overview

Cafe Gokulam is a beloved local restaurant known for its authentic South Indian cuisine and homely atmosphere. They needed a website that captures the warmth of their physical space and makes their menu easily accessible to customers.

The goal was to create an appetizing digital experience that reflects the cafe's identity — warm, traditional, and welcoming — while driving foot traffic and online orders.

Key Objectives

  • Create a warm, appetizing visual design
  • Make the menu easy to browse and navigate
  • Reflect the cafe's traditional identity digitally
  • Drive walk-in traffic through appealing food imagery
  • Provide essential info: menu, timings, location, contact
The Challenge

Problem Statement

01

Digital Visibility

Despite being a popular local eatery, Cafe Gokulam had no web presence. Customers searching online for "cafes near me" couldn't discover them, losing out to competitors with websites.

02

Menu Accessibility

The physical menu was the only way to see offerings. Customers couldn't browse the menu before visiting, making it harder to decide and recommend to others.

03

Brand Perception

Without a professional online presence, the cafe's quality was underrepresented. A well-designed website could elevate the brand perception and attract new customer segments.

Research

Research & Discovery

Competitive Analysis

I researched cafe and restaurant websites, focusing on how they present menus and create appetite appeal:

  • Food photography is the single most impactful element — appetizing images drive cravings and visits
  • Warm color palettes (oranges, browns, creams) stimulate appetite and create a cozy feeling
  • Menu organization by categories (starters, mains, beverages) mirrors the physical menu experience
  • Operating hours and location should be instantly visible — these are the top-searched queries

Target Audience

Primary User

Local Regular

Age 20-45, lives or works nearby. Visits frequently for breakfast or lunch. Wants quick access to today's specials, timings, and to share the cafe with friends.

Secondary User

First-Time Visitor

Age 18-40, searching for cafes in the area. Needs to see the menu, prices, ambiance, and reviews before deciding to visit. Arrives via Google or social referrals.

Process

Design Process

01

Understanding the Brand

Studied Cafe Gokulam's identity — the warm interiors, South Indian menu, homely service style. The digital experience needed to feel like walking into the cafe itself.

02

Content Mapping

Identified essential content: Hero with food imagery, Menu with categories, About section with the cafe's story, Gallery, and Contact with map integration.

03

Visual Direction

Chose warm, earthy tones — deep oranges, cream backgrounds, and brown accents. Typography was kept clean and readable with a slightly playful touch to match the cafe's personality.

04

UI Design

Designed the menu section with card-based layouts, food photography, and clear pricing. Created a smooth scrolling experience that guides visitors from hero to menu to contact.

05

Build & Deploy

Developed the responsive website with attention to load speed (optimized images) and accessibility. Deployed on Netlify with a custom domain setup.

Design Decisions

Key Design Decisions

Warm Color Palette

Used earthy oranges, warm browns, and cream tones throughout the design. Research shows warm colors stimulate appetite and create a welcoming, cozy atmosphere digitally.

Food-Forward Hero

The hero section leads with appetizing food photography rather than a generic cafe image. When users land on the page, the food immediately triggers interest and cravings.

Categorized Menu Layout

Organized the menu into clear categories with visual separators. Each item shows the dish name, a brief description, and price — mirroring how a physical menu works.

Clean Typography

Used readable, modern fonts with sufficient size and contrast. For a restaurant site, readability directly impacts usability — especially for menu browsing on mobile.

Location-Focused Footer

Placed a prominent location section with map integration and operating hours. For local restaurants, "where" and "when" are the most commonly searched pieces of information.

Mobile-Optimized Experience

Most cafe searches happen on mobile ("cafes near me"). The design prioritizes touch-friendly interactions, fast-loading images, and easy-to-read menu text on small screens.

Key Learnings

  • Food photography drives engagement: High-quality food images are the most persuasive element on a restaurant website. They make the difference between "maybe" and "let's go."
  • Colors affect appetite: The warm color palette wasn't just aesthetic — it's backed by psychology. Warm tones create appetite appeal and a sense of comfort.
  • Simplicity for local businesses: Local restaurant websites don't need complexity. Clear menu, hours, location, and contact — the basics done beautifully are more effective than feature-rich sites.
  • Typography as experience: The right typeface can evoke tradition, warmth, or modernity. For Cafe Gokulam, clean yet warm typography reinforced the brand's homely character.
