Case Study

Unique Car Accessories

Building a Sleek E-Commerce Experience for Automotive Enthusiasts

Role UI/UX Designer
Timeline 1 Week
Tools Figma, Claude AI
Type E-Commerce
Overview

Project Overview

Unique Car Accessories is a car accessories shop that needed to expand from a physical store to an online presence. They offer a wide range of automotive accessories — from interior upgrades to exterior styling kits.

The goal was to create a sleek, product-focused e-commerce website that makes browsing and discovering car accessories effortless, while building trust and driving sales.

Key Objectives

  • Design a product-focused e-commerce layout
  • Create an intuitive product browsing experience
  • Build trust through professional design and clear CTAs
  • Showcase product categories with visual hierarchy
  • Optimize for mobile shopping behavior
The Challenge

Problem Statement

01

Limited Reach

As a physical store, Unique Car Accessories could only serve walk-in customers. An online store would expand their reach to car enthusiasts across the city and beyond.

02

Product Discovery

With a large inventory of accessories, customers in-store often didn't know what was available. A digital catalog with categories and search would improve product discovery.

03

Trust & Credibility

Online shoppers need assurance about product quality, return policies, and seller legitimacy. Without a professional website, the brand lacked digital credibility.

Research

Research & Discovery

Competitive Analysis

I analyzed successful automotive e-commerce websites to understand what drives purchases:

  • Product imagery is critical — clear, high-resolution product photos from multiple angles drive purchase confidence
  • Category navigation helps users filter through large inventories efficiently
  • Trust signals (warranties, reviews, secure checkout badges) reduce purchase anxiety
  • Clear pricing with prominent "Add to Cart" or "Shop Now" CTAs convert browsers to buyers

Target Audience

Primary User

Car Enthusiast

Age 22-40, owns a car and actively looks for upgrades. Browses accessories for aesthetics and functionality. Compares products across sites before buying. Values detailed specs and reviews.

Secondary User

Practical Car Owner

Age 25-50, needs specific replacement parts or practical accessories (phone mounts, seat covers). Searches with intent, wants to find, verify compatibility, and purchase quickly.

Process

Design Process

01

User Research

Studied how people shop for car accessories online. Key insight: users want to see products on actual cars, not just standalone product shots. Context drives conversion.

02

Information Architecture

Organized products into clear categories: Interior, Exterior, Electronics, Lighting, and Accessories. Designed a navigation system that supports both browsing and searching.

03

Wireframing

Created wireframes for key pages: Homepage, Product Listing, Product Detail, and Contact. Focused on the product card design to balance image, info, and CTA visibility.

04

Visual Design

Chose a dark, automotive-inspired color palette with accent colors for CTAs. The sleek, modern aesthetic appeals to car enthusiasts and communicates quality and professionalism.

05

Development & Launch

Built the responsive e-commerce site with product grids, hover effects, and smooth transitions. Deployed on Netlify for fast global content delivery.

Design Decisions

Key Design Decisions

Dark, Automotive Theme

A dark color scheme with metallic accents mirrors the automotive industry aesthetic. It makes product images pop and creates a premium, showroom-like feel.

Product-First Layout

Products take center stage with large imagery and minimal distractions. Each product card shows the image, name, price, and a clear CTA — the essentials for quick decision-making.

Category-Based Navigation

Organized products into intuitive categories with visual icons. Users can quickly drill down to their specific need without browsing through irrelevant items.

Clear Pricing Display

Prices are prominently shown on every product card and detail page. No hidden costs or "contact for price" — transparency builds trust in e-commerce.

Responsive Product Grid

Product grid adapts from 4 columns on desktop to 2 on tablet and 1 on mobile. The card size and touch targets are optimized for comfortable mobile shopping.

Strong Call-to-Actions

Bright, contrasting CTA buttons ("Shop Now", "View Details") stand out against the dark background. Strategically placed throughout the page to capture intent at every scroll point.

Outcome

Key Learnings

  • Product images sell: In e-commerce, the product image does most of the selling. Investing in clear, contextual product photography is the highest-ROI design decision.
  • Simplify the decision path: Too many options overwhelm users. Good category structure and filtering reduce cognitive load and help users find what they need faster.
  • Trust through design: A professional, polished website design is itself a trust signal. For a new online store, the website quality is a proxy for product quality in the customer's mind.
  • Mobile-first for e-commerce: A growing majority of online shopping happens on mobile. Designing for mobile first ensures the critical purchase path works on the most-used device.
